About

Jordan Ellis

PhD Candidate, Cognitive Science — Riverton University

I study how people build mental models of unfamiliar systems — from software interfaces to public transit maps — and use that work to design tools that make complex information easier to reason about. Before Riverton, I worked as a UX researcher at a healthcare startup, which is where most of my research questions still come from.

jordan.ellis@riverton.edu Google Scholar GitHub Twitter / X
Currently: writing my dissertation on mental-model formation in novice programmers, and TA-ing Introduction to Human-Computer Interaction (Fall 2026).
Research interests
FOCUS

Mental models & system legibility

How novices form, revise, and misapply mental models when learning new technical systems, and what that means for interface design.

FOCUS

Human-centered evaluation methods

Mixed-method study design for HCI research — combining think-aloud protocols with behavioral logging to triangulate what people actually understand.

FOCUS

Educational tooling

Designing and testing learning environments for early computer-science education, particularly around debugging and error recovery.
