About

Asha Sreekala Viswanathan

PhD Candidate, TRIP Centre — IIT Delhi

I am a PhD candidate working on air pollution, road safety and transport policy evaluation at the Transportation Research and Injury Prevention Centre (TRIPC), Indian Institute of Technology Delhi. My research lies at the intersection of environmental health, transport systems, and quantitative decision science. I develop computational models that connect human activity, environmental processes, and health outcomes, with a particular emphasis on uncertainty quantification and evidence-based policy evaluation. My interests include air pollution, road safety, Bayesian decision analysis, causal inference, geospatial modelling, and scientific computing.

ashasv@tripp.iitd.ac.in Google Scholar GitHub
Currently: writing my dissertation on developing prospective evaluation methods for transport policies.
Current Research Interests
FOCUS

Environmental systems and public health

FOCUS

Decision-making under uncertainty

Developing quantitative methods to support policy decisions by combining Bayesian inference, uncertainty analysis, and the value of information.

FOCUS

Causal inference and scientific computing
